## Station
Callsign is 2D0PEY — Intermediate licence, Isle of Man, 100W maximum power.
Grid square is IO74RE, located at approximately 54.2°N 4.6°W on the Isle of Man.
Isle of Man is a Crown Dependency, not part of the UK but uses the same 3-tier licence structure: Foundation=25W, Intermediate=100W, Full=1kW.
The operator will NOT be upgrading to Full licence — cost and losing the 2D0PEY callsign are the reasons.
HF rig is an Icom IC-7300 covering HF and 6m.
VHF/UHF rig is an Icom IC-9700 covering 2m, 70cm, and 23cm (10W on 23cm).
HF antenna is a Hustler 6-BTV vertical trap antenna covering 10m, 15m, 20m, 30m, 40m, and 75m.
The Hustler 6-BTV has approximately 36 buried radials, 8-10 metres long. Buried radials do not need to be resonant — they act as a non-resonant ground screen. 36 radials is well into diminishing returns territory.
2m antenna is a PowAbeam UKAC144-8: 8 element Yagi, 13.2 dBi, 3.4m boom. Designed for central UK UKAC contests — the broad pattern may not be ideal for IoM geography.
70cm antenna is a PowAbeam UKAC432-15: 15 element Yagi, 16.7 dBi, 28.2 dB F/B ratio, 3.1m boom.
No 23cm antenna yet — considering the WIMO SHF 2367 (67el, 19.9 dBd, 5m boom).

## Operating style
Primary modes are FT8 and FT4 for HF digital. Also operates SSB and CW. Does NOT exclusively use digital modes.
Typically operates at around 70W on HF (below the 100W licence limit).
20m is the primary DX band — opens well to North America and further in the afternoon/evening.
40m is good for European contacts in the evening and short-skip to UK/Ireland.
20m tends to go quiet later in the evening as the long-path to NA closes.
On a good evening session (16 March 2026): 50 QSOs, 22 DXCC entities confirmed, working Australia and USA easily on 20m FT4 at ~70W.
